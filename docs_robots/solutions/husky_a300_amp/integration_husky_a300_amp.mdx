### Eurorack Interface, For Custom Electronics

The Husky AMP's enclosure includes a metal rack with many M3×0.5 threaded holes.
The spacing of these holes conform to the Eurorack 3U standard.
You can create your own modules to interface with this rack, or purchase modules from Clearpath.

The [STEP model](#step-model) includes this rack, as well as a volume representing the modules that are required for the Husky AMP's sensors.

### Cable Routings {#cable-routings}

The AMP's enclosure has brushes on both ends, allowing you to route cables outside the enclosure.
There are 3 layers of brushes per side, to limit water ingress.
Cables should be woven through the brushes sideways, to prevent a straight water ingress point.

:::tip

Test your integration for ingress points by placing a light inside the enclosure, and then looking for light bleed near cables.
Adjust your routings till you are confident the integration will prevent water splash ingress in accordance with IPx4.

:::

:::info

The enclosure has threaded holes to mount an [Icotek KEL-U 24&#124;10 V2A _(54241.200)_](https://ca.rs-online.com/product/icotek/54241-200/72429591/).
This Icotek housing replaces the AMP enclosure's 3 layers of brushes.
You can then use Icotek split grommets to retain cables.

This is not required for most IP54 applications.
Brushes are the default configuration for Husky AMP as they simplify integrations.

:::

### Aluminum Extrusions

There are two aluminum extrusions on the top of the Husky AMP's enclosure.
The centre of these extrusions are spaced 340 mm apart.
The extrusions accept Misumi 5 Series T-slot Nuts.
These are intended as an interface for light components, since the rails are attached with six M3×0.5 screws.

Here are some of the Misumi T-slot fasteners we use:

| Clearpath Item | Misumi Item  | Description                             |
| :------------- | :----------- | :-------------------------------------- |
| 021464         | HNTASN5-5    | M5×0.8 nut, without retention           |

| 026955         | SHNTAP5-5    | M5×0.8 nut, with leaf spring retention  |
| 001018         | HATLSN5-4-12 | M5×0.8 stud, without retention          |

### Large Component Mounts, Like Arms / Manipulators

There is a grid of Ø5 mm holes on the top of the enclosure, near the front and rear edges.
These holes have a pitch of 80 mm, same as Clearpath's PACS system.
The holes are located near the enclosure's weld seams, to improve stiffness between the large component and the Husky's wheels.

To make a Husky AMP, we take a Husky A300, remove the Top Plate, and then install an enclosure on top of the base robot.
We added a connector bulkhead between the base robot and the enclosure to keep cable routings tidy, and make the maintenence process quick.
The connectors on this bulkhead are extension cables from existing [Husky A300 interfaces](../../outdoor_robots/husky/a300/integration_husky/#system-interface-circuit-board-connector-summary), which are listed below.

:::info

We add Bluetooth and Wireless E-stop antenna cables on top of the antenna bulkhead using cable ties.
This keeps the antennas electrically isolated from the robot's chassis.

:::

:::tip

You can add custom cables on top of the bulkhead using tie wraps.
You should include an inline connector at this interface for your custom cables, so the enclosure can easily be removed from the base Husky A300 for maintenance.

:::

### Fusing

The AMP enclosure does not include any new fuses.
Refer to Husky A300's [Electrical Integration](../../outdoor_robots/husky/a300/integration_husky/#electrical-integration) section for fuse locations, and methods to change them.
