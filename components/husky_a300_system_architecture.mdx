### System Architecture

Husky is built around a computer running Ubuntu—_(Intel-based computer)_—paired with a 32-bit microcontroller unit, _(MCU)_.

[//]: <> (TODO, add diagram)

The MCU handles low level hardware functions such as power supply control, light control, fan control, screen control, and hardware monitoring.
The computer and MCU communicate with Ethernet.

[//]: <> (TODO, add diagram)

The motor controllers and batteries connect to the MCU through indiviual CANbuses.
The MCU's firmware then passes this through to the computer over Ethernet.
The computer is configured with SocketCAN to interface with these networks.

[//]: <> (TODO, add diagram)

The computer then connects to other devices through Ethernet and USB.
Most Huskies include an IMU connected to the primary computer with USB.
Husky AMP connects lidar and camera through these interfaces.
