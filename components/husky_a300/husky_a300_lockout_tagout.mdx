Lockout procedures remove potentional energy from machines as we perform maintenance.
This reduces risk of harm while performing maintenance.
The Husky's main source of potential energy is the batteries.
We cannot completely discharge these batteries without damaging them, but we can disconnect them from all the robot's electronics.


<details>
  <summary>To disconnect the batteries, and lockout the machine:</summary>

  1.  If applicable; move the Husky away from its _Wireless Charger_.
  2.  Open the Husky's _Rear Charge Port Door_.
  3.  Disconnect the _Manual Charger_.
  4.  Flip the _Circuit Breaker_ to the OFF position.
  5.  Confirm that the robot will not turn on, by pressing and holding the ON-OFF button for 0.5 seconds.

    Now that you have isolated the batteries; you can lockout the robot.
    We do this by closing the _Rear Charge Port Door_, and then locking it to the robot's chassis.

    :::note

    The lockout pin and scissors shown in the image are not included with a standard Husky order.
    Any pin or hasp that prevents the _Rear Charge Port Door_ from opening should be acceptable.

    If you want the same hardware shown in this image, contact [Clearpath's Support Team](#support), and ask about the Lockout Kit—_032200_.

    :::

</details>
